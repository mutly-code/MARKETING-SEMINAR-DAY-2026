Messages

Dear Students,

As we gather for today's Seminar under the theme "Your Brand, Your Balance: Thriving as a Marketing Graduate," celebrate the journey that has equipped you with timeless marketing fundamentals—consumer insights, strategic branding, and creative storytelling. These core principles form the bedrock of your personal and professional brand, enabling you to thrive amid rapid changes. While AI offers powerful tools for data analysis, idea generation, and efficiency, resist the temptation to let it replace your ingenuity. Instead, wield AI as an assistant that amplifies your expertise, ensuring your work reflects authentic human judgment and originality.

I've noticed inspiring efforts from many in the cohort who poured heart into their projects, communicating ideas with clarity and passion. Yet, for others, challenges in effective communication dimmed their potential, reminding us that strong messaging is as vital in marketing as it is in collaboration. Hone these skills deliberately: practice concise pitches, active listening, and audience-tailored narratives to elevate your voice. By prioritizing clear, ethical communication, you'll build trust and stand out in a crowded marketplace.

At the centre of it all must remain strong ethics—guiding your decisions, innovations, and interactions. Let integrity balance your brand, turning challenges into triumphs. Embrace your seminar journey and today's conference as your launchpad to thrive! Remember that you are resilient! May God continue to bless you.

— Suzette Townsend, Lead Facilitator

Dear Marketing Students,

Remember that some of your greatest lessons in life as human beings and professionals were learnt within the four walls of this noble institution. I hope that you will stay committed to the core values and attitudes taught and demonstrated as you walked through the corridors of UTech.

Remember to maintain a healthy work-life balance and please don't leave God at the door. Walk Good!

— Olubusola Akinladejo, Acting Dean, College of Business and Management, School of Business Administration

Congratulations to the Marketing Seminar Class of 2025/2026 on reaching this stage in your educational journey. Commit yourselves to developing not just your career and your ability to earn a living, but also to developing yourself and your ability to build a life. Cultivate a meaningful life that is of value to others in society. Use all facets of your being to serve your country well.

Remember Jamaica's National Pledge in which we promise to give the love and loyalty of our hearts, the wisdom and courage of our minds, and the strength and vigour of our bodies in the service of others. This is particularly important now as many Jamaicans are trying to recover from the devastation caused by Hurricane Melissa in October 2025.

In this seminar module, you have developed skills in research, volunteerism, critical thinking, conflict resolution, etiquette and decorum, business communication, marketing, logistics, budgeting, and meeting and events management. Put these skills to work in helping our national effort in "Rebuilding Lives, Restoring Hope." May God continue to bless you.

— Celia McKoy, Head of School, School of Business Administration

Commendations on the culmination of a tremendous journey. You are on the cusp of launching out into the world to make your indelible mark. You have been prepared, so take the knowledge and soar.

— Latoya Saddler, Lecturer

"The greatest glory in living lies not in never falling, but in rising every time we fall." — Nelson Mandela

The theme for this year's 44th annual Marketing Seminar, "Your Brand, Your Balance: Thriving as a Marketing Graduate," highlights the essence of what this course has represented for the past 44 years. The Marketing Seminar course is a practical module that allows students to apply knowledge gained from earlier and current marketing modules to execute real events, such as Seminar Day. Upon the successful completion of Marketing Seminar, each student will be able to:

  • Understand their strengths and weaknesses, which act as a guide to building their brand.
  • Experience corporate life and what it means to meet tight deadlines, work as a team, and execute corporate events; this requires balance.
  • Learn how to grow as professionals by overcoming obstacles while building their brand and balancing a demanding professional life; that is thriving.

The marketing graduate who properly understands this theme and successfully passes this course has already gained an advantage in starting a marketing career and is on the path to becoming an excellent marketing professional. For those who still struggle with this concept, let's take a closer look. The transition from the classroom to the boardroom is more than a change of scenery; it is the moment you become your own most important client. To thrive in the corporate world, you must first understand your brand and how to maintain balance.

You need to understand your narrative (your brand), and Marketing Seminar has been a major help in shaping this. As you compete against numerous graduates, your brand allows you to stand out; it is your professional heartbeat. This is the intersection of your unique skill set and the specific value you intend to bring to an organization. You must also protect your pace (your balance). Marketing requires constant attention, focus, and discipline; however, to perform at your best, you still need downtime. Balance is not about resting alone; it is about strategic recovery. Treat your energy as a finite resource: a well-rested marketer is a more innovative strategist.

Thus, thriving does not mean doing everything; it means doing the right things with a clear identity. When you align who you are with how you work, you don't just survive the early years of your career—you lead them.

— Michael Lee, Facilitator
